    Compensation & Commission Structure#

    Overview#

    Compensation design that incentivizes new business, retention, cross-selling, and platform adoption.

    Commission Structure#

    Personal Lines#

    Auto Insurance

    • New business: 12-15% of annual premium
    • Renewal: 10-12% of annual premium
    • Contingency bonus: Based on loss ratio and volume

    Home Insurance

    • New business: 15-18% of annual premium
    • Renewal: 12-15% of annual premium
    • Contingency bonus: Based on loss ratio and volume

    Umbrella

    • New business: 15% of annual premium
    • Renewal: 15% of annual premium

    Boat/Recreational

    • New business: 15-18% of annual premium
    • Renewal: 15% of annual premium

    Commercial Lines#

    BOP (Business Owner Policy)

    • New business: 15-20% of annual premium
    • Renewal: 12-15% of annual premium

    Apartments/Multi-family

    • New business: 15-20% of annual premium
    • Renewal: 12-15% of annual premium
    • Higher complexity may warrant higher commission

    Life Insurance#

    Term Life

    • Year 1: 80-100% of annual premium
    • Renewals: 2-5% (carrier dependent)

    Permanent Life (Whole, Universal)

    • Year 1: 80-100% of first year premium
    • Renewals: 2-5% ongoing

    Final Expense

    • Year 1: 80-100% of annual premium
    • Renewals: 5-8% (higher than term due to persistency importance)

    Life & Legacy Platform Commissions#

    Initial Setup#

    • $50 one-time bonus per enrolled household
    • Paid upon completion of client onboarding

    Recurring Revenue#

    • 20% of monthly subscription ($2/month per $9.99 subscriber)
    • Or 30% if agent provides tier-1 support

    Retention Bonus#

    • Additional $50 for each household that remains active after 12 months
    • $25 for each subsequent annual renewal

    Why this matters: Platform creates recurring revenue stream vs one-time life insurance commission.

    Bonus Structure#

    Multi-line Bonus#

    • 3+ policies per household: $100 bonus
    • 5+ policies per household: $250 bonus
    • Applied once per household at initial sale

    Production Bonuses (Monthly)#

    Tier 1 - $10k+ new premium/month

    • Base commission only

    Tier 2 - $25k+ new premium/month

    • 2% bonus on all new business

    Tier 3 - $50k+ new premium/month

    • 5% bonus on all new business
    • Quarterly team dinner

    Tier 4 - $100k+ new premium/month

    • 10% bonus on all new business
    • Annual trip for two

    Retention Bonus (Annual)#

    Based on persistency ratio:

    • 85-89%: $1,000 bonus
    • 90-94%: $2,500 bonus
    • 95%+: $5,000 bonus

    Referral Bonus#

    • $50 per qualified referral that quotes
    • $100 additional if referral binds
    • No limit on number of referrals

    Salary vs Commission Models#

    Option A: Pure Commission (Independent Agent)#

    • 100% commission-based
    • Higher commission rates
    • Agent owns book of business
    • Best for experienced agents with existing clients

    Option B: Base + Commission (Employee Agent)#

    • $40,000-60,000 base salary
    • Reduced commission rates (70% of standard)
    • Agency owns book of business
    • Best for newer agents building book

    Option C: Draw vs Commission#

    • $3,000/month draw
    • Offset by commissions earned
    • After 12 months, switch to pure commission or salary+commission
    • Best for experienced agents in transition

    Sample Earnings Scenarios#

    Scenario 1: New Agent, Year 1#

    • 50 personal lines households ($800 avg premium) = $6,000 commission
    • 10 life insurance policies ($2,000 avg premium, 90% commission) = $18,000
    • 5 platform enrollments × $50 = $250
    • Platform recurring (5 × $2/mo × 12) = $120
    • Total Year 1: $24,370 commission
    • Plus base salary (if employee): $40,000-60,000

    Scenario 2: Established Agent, Year 3#

    • 150 personal lines households ($800 avg premium) = $18,000 new + $14,400 renewal
    • 30 life insurance policies = $54,000 new + $2,000 renewal
    • 20 platform households × $50 = $1,000
    • Platform recurring (20 × $2/mo × 12) = $480
    • Multi-line bonuses = $2,000
    • Production bonus (Tier 2) = $1,440
    • Retention bonus = $2,500
    • Total Year 3: $95,820 commission

    Scenario 3: Top Producer, Year 5#

    • 300 personal lines households = $36,000 new + $28,800 renewal
    • 60 life insurance policies = $108,000 new + $4,000 renewal
    • 50 platform households = $2,500 setup + $1,200/year recurring
    • Bonuses: $5,000 (retention) + $3,000 (production) + $4,000 (multi-line)
    • Total Year 5: $192,500 commission

    Platform Revenue Comparison#

    Traditional Life Agent#

    • Sells $2,000 term policy
    • Earns $1,800 year one
    • Earns $80/year renewal (if policy stays in force)
    • 10-year value: ~$2,520

    Platform-Enabled Agent#

    • Sells same $2,000 term policy = $1,800
    • Enrolls in platform = $50 setup bonus
    • Earns $24/year platform recurring
    • Client stays 10 years (higher retention due to platform engagement)
    • 10-year value: ~$2,390 insurance + $290 platform = $2,680
    • Plus: Client more likely to buy additional products due to ongoing relationship

    Performance Metrics Tied to Compensation#

    Required Minimums (to maintain employment/appointment)#

    • $10k/month new business average (after first 6 months)
    • 85%+ persistency ratio
    • 90%+ client satisfaction score
    • Compliance: Zero E&O claims or licensing violations

    Growth Incentives#

    • 20%+ year-over-year growth: Extra week PTO
    • Top producer: President’s Club trip
    • Platform adoption leader: Bonus week salary

    Expense Reimbursements#

    • Licensing and continuing education: 100%
    • E&O insurance: 100%
    • Lead costs: 50% (up to $500/month)
    • CRM and tech tools: 100%
    • Marketing materials: 100%
    • Client entertainment: Up to $200/month
    • Mileage: IRS standard rate
    • Phone: $75/month stipend

    Benefits (if employee model)#

    • Health insurance: 75% employer paid
    • Dental and vision: 50% employer paid
    • 401k: 3% match
    • PTO: 2 weeks year 1, 3 weeks year 2+
    • Paid holidays: 8 days
    • Sick leave: 5 days

    Chargebacks and Clawbacks#

    Policy Cancellation#

    • If policy cancels within 6 months: 100% commission chargeback
    • If policy cancels 6-12 months: 50% commission chargeback
    • After 12 months: No chargeback

    Platform Cancellation#

    • If client cancels within 3 months: Setup bonus clawed back
    • Recurring commission stops when client cancels (no chargeback on past earnings)

    Mitigation#

    • Agent must attempt to save policy before allowing cancellation
    • Proper underwriting and sales process reduces chargebacks
    • Platform engagement (annual reviews) reduces cancellations

    Career Progression#

    Associate Agent (Year 1)#

    • Base + reduced commission
    • Mentorship from senior agent
    • Learning phase

    Agent (Year 2+)#

    • Full commission structure
    • Own book of business (if independent model)
    • Expected to hit production minimums

    Senior Agent (Year 5+)#

    • Higher commission rates for top producers
    • May receive leads from agency
    • Mentorship bonus for training new agents

    Agency Partner (Potential)#

    • Equity stake in agency
    • Override on other agents’ production
    • Strategic decision-making role
