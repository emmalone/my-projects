    Comprehensive Marketing Plan#

    Executive Summary#

    KLM’s marketing strategy focuses on differentiation through our life & legacy platform while maintaining competitive positioning in traditional insurance lines.

    Market Analysis#

    Competitive Landscape#

    Traditional Insurance

    • Highly competitive, price-driven market
    • Online direct competitors (Geico, Progressive)
    • Local independent agents
    • Captive agents (State Farm, Allstate)

    Our Position

    • Independent agency advantages (carrier choice, advocacy)
    • Local service vs call center
    • Life & legacy platform as unique differentiator

    Life & Legacy Platform

    • Few direct competitors
    • Estate planning attorneys (different scope)
    • Standalone apps (Everplans, MyDirectives)
    • Our advantage: bundled with insurance relationship

    Target Market Segmentation#

    Tier 1 (Highest Value)#

    • Ages 45-65, income $150k+
    • Current life insurance + estate planning needs
    • Likely to buy multiple products
    • High lifetime value

    Tier 2 (Growth)#

    • Small business owners (commercial + personal)
    • Affluent families under 45 (building wealth)
    • Retirees with legacy concerns

    Tier 3 (Volume)#

    • First-time homeowners
    • Young families
    • Budget-conscious insurance shoppers

    Marketing Objectives (12-month)#

    1. Generate 500 qualified leads/month across all lines
    2. Launch life & legacy platform to 100 pilot clients
    3. Build email list to 2,000 subscribers
    4. Achieve 50% brand awareness in target zip codes
    5. Recruit 5 new agents to platform

    Marketing Mix#

    Digital Marketing (60% of budget)#

    Facebook/Instagram Ads (25%)

    • Lead generation campaigns
    • Retargeting sequences
    • Brand awareness

    Google Ads (20%)

    • Search ads (high intent keywords)
    • Display remarketing
    • YouTube pre-roll

    SEO & Content (10%)

    • Blog posts (2x/month)
    • FAQ pages
    • Local SEO optimization
    • Google Business Profile

    Email Marketing (5%)

    • Lead nurture sequences
    • Client newsletters
    • Product launch campaigns

    Traditional Marketing (20% of budget)#

    Direct Mail

    • Postcard campaigns to target zip codes
    • Annual review reminders
    • Life event triggers (home sales, new business filings)

    Local Sponsorships

    • Youth sports teams
    • Community events
    • Chamber of Commerce

    Networking

    • BNI or similar referral groups
    • Professional associations
    • Estate planning attorney partnerships

    Referral Programs (10% of budget)#

    Client Referral Program

    • Gift card for qualified referrals
    • Extra rewards for multi-line referrals

    Professional Referrals

    • CPA partnerships (tax time life insurance discussions)
    • Attorney partnerships (estate planning)
    • Financial advisors (insurance reviews)
    • Mortgage brokers (new homeowners)

    Public Relations (10% of budget)#

    • Local media relationships
    • Expert commentary on insurance topics
    • Press releases for major announcements
    • Community involvement stories

    Messaging Framework#

    Core Brand Messages#

    For Traditional Insurance: “Independent advice, local service, competitive pricing”

    For Life & Legacy Platform: “Insurance protects your family financially. We help them know what to do next.”

    Unique Value Propositions#

    1. Choice: Access to multiple carriers vs single company
    2. Advocacy: We work for you, not the insurance company
    3. Comprehensive: Planning beyond the policy
    4. Relationships: Same agent for years, not call centers
    5. Innovation: Modern tools with personal service

    Campaign Calendar#

    Q1 (Jan-Mar)#

    • New year planning campaign
    • Life insurance awareness (February)
    • Home policy reviews (winter claims season)
    • Life & legacy platform soft launch to existing clients

    Q2 (Apr-Jun)#

    • Tax season estate planning
    • Home buying season
    • Boat insurance (spring)
    • Platform beta expansion

    Q3 (Jul-Sep)#

    • Summer safety
    • Back-to-school (teen drivers)
    • Life insurance awareness month (September)
    • Platform public launch

    Q4 (Oct-Dec)#

    • Small business month
    • Holiday safety
    • Year-end financial planning
    • Platform agent recruitment

    Budget Allocation#

    Total Annual Budget: $60,000

    • Digital Marketing: $36,000
    • Traditional Marketing: $12,000
    • Referral Programs: $6,000
    • PR & Events: $6,000

    Monthly: $5,000 average

    Measurement & Analytics#

    Key Performance Indicators (KPIs)#

    Lead Generation

    • Total leads per month
    • Cost per lead by channel
    • Lead quality score (agent rating)

    Conversion

    • Lead to quote: 40% goal
    • Quote to bind: 25% goal
    • Overall conversion: 10% goal

    Life & Legacy Platform

    • Trial signups per month
    • Trial to paid conversion
    • Monthly recurring revenue (MRR)
    • Churn rate

    Brand Metrics

    • Website traffic
    • Social media engagement
    • Email open rates (18%+ goal)
    • Net Promoter Score

    ROI

    • Revenue per marketing dollar: 5:1 goal
    • Customer lifetime value
    • Payback period by channel

    Reporting#

    • Weekly: Lead volume and cost by channel
    • Monthly: Full dashboard review
    • Quarterly: Strategy adjustment based on results

    Marketing Technology Stack#

    1. CRM: Salesforce or HubSpot
    2. Email: Mailchimp or ActiveCampaign
    3. Website: WordPress or custom
    4. Analytics: Google Analytics 4
    5. Ad Management: Facebook Ads Manager, Google Ads
    6. Landing Pages: Unbounce or LeadPages
    7. Call Tracking: CallRail
    8. Social Media: Hootsuite or Buffer

    Compliance & Legal#

    • All marketing materials reviewed for insurance compliance
    • Disclaimers on product comparisons
    • Privacy policy and TCPA compliance
    • E&O insurance coverage for marketing
    • State-specific regulations adherence

    Competitive Advantages to Emphasize#

    1. Life & legacy platform (no competitor has this bundled with insurance)
    2. Independent agency (carrier choice and advocacy)
    3. Local service (vs online-only or call centers)
    4. Multi-generational planning (insurance + estate + elder care)
    5. Technology-enabled but relationship-driven

    Risks & Mitigation#

    Risk: Life & legacy platform messaging confuses traditional insurance buyers Mitigation: Separate campaigns and landing pages by audience

    Risk: Digital ad costs increase, reducing ROI Mitigation: Diversify channels, build organic presence, focus on referrals

    Risk: Competitors copy life & legacy approach Mitigation: First-mover advantage, build brand loyalty, continuous innovation

    Future Opportunities#

    • Podcast sponsorships (estate planning, small business)
    • Webinar series on life planning topics
    • YouTube channel (educational content)
    • Strategic partnerships with eldercare providers
    • Affiliate program for platform
