    Survivorship Playbook for the Family#

    Status: Phase 1 - Core Platform Module

    Overview#

    A “when I die, do this” interactive guide aimed at the surviving spouse or children. This is sold at policy issue time and creates long-term stickiness with the family.

    Purpose#

    Loved ones can navigate the first 90 days after death with less stress and fewer missed benefits.

    Features#

    Immediate Action Checklist (First 72 Hours)#

    • Notify immediate family
    • Contact employer (if applicable)
    • Arrange for care of dependents/pets
    • Secure the home
    • Contact funeral home
    • Locate important documents

    First Week Tasks#

    • Obtain death certificates (order 10-15 copies)
    • Notify life insurance company
    • Contact Social Security Administration
    • Notify employer and HR department
    • Contact deceased’s attorney
    • Notify financial institutions (flag accounts, don’t close yet)
    • Forward mail to executor/survivor
    • Arrange funeral/memorial service

    First Month Tasks#

    • File life insurance claims
    • Meet with estate attorney
    • Review will and trust documents
    • Inventory assets and debts
    • Notify credit bureaus (prevent identity theft)
    • Contact pension administrator
    • Apply for survivor benefits (Social Security, VA, etc)
    • Review and adjust household budget
    • Change utilities and services to survivor’s name
    • Update beneficiaries on survivor’s accounts

    First 90 Days#

    • File for probate (if required)
    • Pay outstanding debts
    • File final tax returns
    • Close or transfer accounts
    • Cancel subscriptions and memberships
    • Retitle assets (home, vehicles)
    • Update estate plan for survivor
    • Review insurance needs for survivor
    • Consider grief counseling or support groups

    Contact Directory#

    Pre-populated list of who to call:

    • Insurance agent
    • Life insurance company claims number
    • Attorney
    • Financial advisor
    • Accountant
    • Funeral home
    • Employer HR contact
    • Social Security Administration
    • Medicare
    • Veterans Affairs
    • Banks and financial institutions
    • Utilities
    • Mortgage company

    Where Things Are Located#

    • Safe deposit box location and key
    • Important documents
    • Insurance policies
    • Financial accounts
    • Digital assets and passwords
    • Titles and deeds
    • Tax returns

    Financial Snapshot#

    • Life insurance policies and amounts
    • Bank accounts and balances (approximate)
    • Investment accounts
    • Retirement accounts
    • Debts and liabilities
    • Monthly expenses to maintain
    • Expected survivor benefits

    Personal Wishes#

    • Funeral/memorial preferences
    • Burial or cremation
    • Service type and location
    • Music, readings, speakers
    • Charitable donations in lieu of flowers
    • Obituary information

    User Experience#

    Setup (by client while living)#

    1. Complete interactive questionnaire
    2. Upload or link to documents
    3. Add contacts and phone numbers
    4. Record personal wishes
    5. Share access credentials with trusted family member

    Activation (after death)#

    1. Family member logs in or receives access
    2. System provides step-by-step checklist
    3. Tasks can be marked complete
    4. Reminders and timeframes provided
    5. Contact information readily available
    6. Documents accessible instantly

    Emotional Design#

    This is a sensitive product that will be used during grief. Design must be:

    • Clear and simple (reduce cognitive load)
    • Compassionate in tone
    • Mobile-friendly (used from anywhere)
    • Printable (some prefer paper checklist)
    • Shareable (multiple family members can collaborate)

    Why Clients Pay#

    Reduces family stress during worst time. Ensures nothing is forgotten or missed. Loved ones feel supported and guided rather than overwhelmed.

    Technical Requirements#

    • Emergency access protocols
    • Death verification process
    • Secure but accessible to family
    • Document attachment to each task
    • Progress tracking
    • Collaborative features (assign tasks to family members)
    • Reminder system

    Pricing#

    Included with Digital Life Vault. Strong retention tool as clients update it over time.

    Success Metrics#

    • % of vault subscribers who complete playbook
    • Average time to complete setup
    • Updates per year (shows engagement)
    • Family member feedback (post-death survey)
    • Claim completion rates (faster/more complete)

    Marketing Angle#

    “Your life insurance pays money, but who tells your family what to do with it? We do.”

    Competitive Advantage#

    No direct competitors bundle this with life insurance. Most estate planning tools stop at the legal documents.

    Agent Training#

    Agents need talking points:

    • “This ensures your family knows exactly what to do”
    • “You’re not just leaving money, you’re leaving instructions”
    • “This is like leaving a roadmap when your family needs it most”
    • “It takes 20 minutes to set up, but saves your family weeks of stress”
